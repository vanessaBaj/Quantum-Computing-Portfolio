## 🧭 Research Abstract

This portfolio presents computational investigations of quantum mechanical systems using numerical spectral methods, operator discretization, and scientific computing techniques.

The research focuses on solving quantum eigenvalue problems in regimes where analytical solutions are not available. The central approach involves transforming continuous quantum operators into discrete matrix representations and solving the resulting eigenvalue problems numerically.

A key objective is to bridge theoretical quantum mechanics with computational simulation, enabling physically consistent numerical models for quantum systems under various potentials and perturbations.



## 🎯 Research Problem

Many quantum systems of physical and theoretical interest—particularly those involving complex potentials, external fields, or boundary constraints—do not admit closed-form analytical solutions.

This work addresses the following fundamental question:

**How can quantum Hamiltonians be discretized and solved numerically while preserving essential physical properties such as Hermiticity, spectral stability, and eigenstate orthogonality?**

## 🧪 Current Computational Projects

### 1. 1D Schrödinger Equation Solver

A numerical framework for solving the time-independent Schrödinger equation using a matrix Hamiltonian formulation.

**Methodology:**
- Finite difference discretization of the second derivative operator  
- Construction of a Hermitian Hamiltonian matrix  
- Numerical diagonalization for eigenvalue computation  
- Reconstruction of eigenfunctions (wavefunctions)  
- Probability density visualization  

**Key Outputs:**
- Discrete energy spectrum  
- Corresponding eigenstates ψ(x)  
- Numerical approximation of bound states  



### 2. Quantum Harmonic Oscillator Simulation

A computational study of the quantum harmonic oscillator as a benchmark system in quantum mechanics.

**Methodology:**
- Operator-based Hamiltonian formulation  
- Numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian  
- Extraction of quantized energy levels  
- Visualization of eigenfunctions and nodal structure  

**Key Outputs:**
- Quantized energy spectrum  
- Wavefunction parity structure  
- Comparison with analytical solutions

To ensure physical and numerical consistency, all simulations are evaluated using:

- Normalization checks of eigenfunctions
